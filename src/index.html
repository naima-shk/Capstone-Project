SELF_AWARENESS





DESCRIPTION


Self-Awareness involves being aware of the various aspects of self-involvement that include traits, behaviors, and feelings. In fact, it is a state of mind where the focus is on yourself. Humans are born with a perfectly sound mind. However, research has also found that infants have a basic sense of self-worth. An example of self-awareness is your ability to share your beliefs with others. It helps us become better decision-makers It gives us more confidence.

Self-Awareness is the ability to tune in to your feelings, thoughts, and actions. Being self-aware also means being able to recognize how other people see you. People who are self-aware recognize their strengths and their challenges.




EMPATHY







DESCRIPTION


Empathy is the ability to empathize with other people's feelings, to see things from their point of view, and to imagine yourself in their place. is a powerful concern for the well-being and happiness of others. There are several types of empathy a person may experience:

  • Affected Empathy involves the ability to understand another person's feelings and respond appropriately.
  • Somatic sensitivity involves having some form of physical response to responding to another person's experience. People sometimes feel what someone else is feeling.
  • Cognitive Empathy involves being able to understand another person's state of mind and what they can think of in response to a situation.

    • DECISION MAKING






    DESCRIPTION


    Decision Making is the process in which one has to make a rational and explainable choice out of more than one option. The term decision making is also referred to as a cognitive process.

    There are three types of decision making: Strategic decision where upper-level management of the organization makes long-term decisions that set the direction for an entire organization. decision where decisions regarding how things will be done are taken. Operational decision where day-to-day decisions are made by the lower-level employees to run the operations of the organization smoothly.

    EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE



    DESCRIPTION


    Emotional Intelligence is (also known as an emotional quotient or EQ) is the ability to understand, use, and manage your emotions in effective ways to relieve stress, communicate effectively, empathize with others, overcome challenges and resolve conflicts. Emotional intelligence helps you to build strong relationships, succeed in school and work, and achieve your career goals and goals. It can also help you to communicate your feelings, turn purpose into action, and make informed decisions about what is most important to you.
